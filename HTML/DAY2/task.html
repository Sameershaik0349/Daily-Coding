7 Wonders

Great Wall of China

The Great Wall of China is one of the longest structures ever built by humans.

It stretches over thousands of kilometers across northern China.

The wall was built mainly to protect China from invasions.

Construction started more than 2,000 years ago.

It was built using stone, brick, and compacted earth.

Many dynasties worked on expanding and repairing it.

Petra

Petra is an ancient city carved into red sandstone cliffs.

It is located in the country of Jordan.

Petra was once a major trading center in ancient times.

The city is famous for its rock-cut architecture.

The Treasury is the most popular structure in Petra.

It was hidden from the world for many centuries.

Petra is also known as the “Rose City

Christ the Redeemer

Christ the Redeemer is a large statue of Jesus Christ.

It is located on Mount Corcovado in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil.

The statue stands with open arms symbolizing peace.

It is made of reinforced concrete and soapstone.

Construction was completed in the year 1931

The statue overlooks the entire city of Rio.

It is a global symbol of Christianity and love.

Machu Picchu

It is one of the most visited tourist places in the world.

Machu Picchu is an ancient Incan city in Peru.

It is located high in the Andes Mountains.

The city was built in the 15th century.

Machu Picchu was abandoned during the Spanish invasion.

It is known for its advanced stone construction.

The site was rediscovered in 1911.

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is a beautiful marble monument in India.

It is located in Agra, on the banks of the Yamuna River.

The monument was built by Emperor Shah Jahan.

It was made in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal.

White marble and precious stones were used in construction.

The Taj Mahal is a symbol of love and beauty.

It is one of the most famous landmarks in the world.

Chichen Itza

Chichen Itza is an ancient Mayan city in Mexico.

It was a major center of politics and religion.

The pyramid El Castillo is the main attraction.

The structure shows advanced astronomical knowledge.

During equinoxes, shadow effects appear on the pyramid.

The city reflects Mayan culture and science.

It is one of the most important archaeological sites in America.

Colosseum

The Colosseum is a massive amphitheater in Rome, Italy.

It was built during the Roman Empire.

Gladiator fights and public shows were held here.

It could hold around 50,000 spectators.

The structure is made of stone and concrete.

It shows the engineering skills of ancient Romans.

The Colosseum is a symbol of Roman history.
